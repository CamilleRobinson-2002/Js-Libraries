Learn about transportation, etiquette, and budgeting.

  • If you plan to travel between cities, the JR Pass is a cost-effective way to ride the
    Shinkansen (bullet train), which connects major destinations across Japan.
  • While credit cards are accepted in many places, smaller shops, restaurants,
    and rural areas often prefer cash, so it's good to carry some yen.
  • Bowing is a common greeting, shoes should be removed before entering homes and
    certain restaurants, and speaking quietly on public transport is expected.
  • Japanese konbini (like 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart)
    offer delicious food, ATMs, and travel essentials, making them a must-visit for travelers.
